Michaelis-Menten Equation

The Michaelis-Menten equation calculator allows you to explore the kinetics of a one-substrate reaction catalyzed by an enzyme. Michaelis-Menten kinetics helps determine the rate of the reaction, substrate concentration, Michaelis constant (Km), and the maximum rate of the reaction (Vmax).


How to use the Michaelis-Menten equation calculator?

To calculate the desired rate of the reaction, V, you will need the following data:

  • Vmax – the maximum rate of the reaction;
  • [S] – the molar concentration of the substrate;
  • Km – the Michaelis constant.

The formula used in the calculator is:

V = (Vmax * [S]) / ([S] + Km)

Here’s what you need to know about the units:

  • Both V and Vmax are written in units of s¯¹ (1/s).
  • If the concentration is entered in molars (M), the unit of V will be M/s.
  • If the concentration is entered in micromolar (μM), the unit of V will be μM/s.
